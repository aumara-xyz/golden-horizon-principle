# Golden Horizon Principle: Core Share Paper

## Abstract

GHP is a variational selection conjecture: among admissible braiding-universal topological boundary architectures, Fibonacci appears as the minimal non-abelian architecture capable of universal computation by braiding alone. The physical conjecture remains open: nature may select observer-boundary architectures by a balance principle whose lowest admissible self-referential computationally universal closure is Fibonacci.

## 1. Central Claim

GHP separates into two layers.

- Mathematical layer: Fibonacci is minimal inside the stated categorical class.
- Physical conjecture layer: observer-boundary architectures may be selected by a variational or balance principle whose first admissible closure is Fibonacci.

The first layer is the spine. The second remains open.

## 3. Mathematical Spine

The Fibonacci anyon theory has two simple objects, `1` and `tau`, with fusion rule

`tau x tau = 1 + tau`.

This yields the quantum-dimension equation

`d_tau^2 = 1 + d_tau`,

whose positive solution is `phi`. The total quantum dimension is

`D^2 = 1 + phi^2 = 2 + phi`.

Within the stated braiding-universal unitary modular category class, Fibonacci is the minimal non-abelian candidate. That is a theorem-grade mathematical statement inside the stated domain. It does not by itself prove physical selection.

## 4. Architecture / Dynamics Split

The golden-chain lesson is that golden structure may govern architecture without forcing all dynamics to be golden. In the relevant regime, Fibonacci information architecture and ordinary low-energy dynamics separate. This keeps GHP from collapsing into the claim that phi should numerically govern everything.

## 5. Physical Conjecture

The open physical conjecture is that admissible observer-boundary architectures may be selected by a balance principle, action, or extremal law favoring the first stable non-abelian braiding-universal closure. Fibonacci is the strongest current candidate for that role. The bridge is not yet derived.

## 8. Primary Falsification Paths

- A smaller braiding-universal UMTC is found in the stated class.
- The selection principle cannot be formalized into a real bridge object.
- Beta-band style tests fail.
- Ordinary low-energy or boring physics limits cannot be recovered.
- Matter embedding remains undefined.

## 10. One-Page Human Summary

GHP asks a narrow but ambitious question: if reality becomes readable only through finite observer boundaries, what is the smallest topological architecture that can support non-abelian braiding and universal computational closure? The current mathematical answer inside the stated class is Fibonacci. The current physical claim is only that nature may prefer such a closure by a deeper balance principle.

That makes GHP strongest as an architectural research program and observer-boundary selection proposal. The theorem-grade core is real. The bridge to physics remains open. The toy models help test language and candidate mechanisms, but they do not count as empirical confirmation.
