## Abstract

The Golden Horizon Principle is a variational observer-boundary conjecture: among admissible braiding-universal topological boundary architectures, Fibonacci appears as the minimal non-abelian architecture capable of universal computation by braiding alone. The theorem-grade mathematical core is categorical. Fibonacci has total quantum dimension

\[
D^2 = 1+\phi^2 = 2+\phi,
\]

and is the smallest braiding-universal non-abelian candidate inside the stated unitary modular-category class.

The physical conjecture is stronger and remains open: nature may select observer-boundary architectures by an extremal or balance principle whose lowest admissible non-abelian, self-referential, computationally universal closure is Fibonacci. The golden-chain computation supports the key architectural lesson: Fibonacci structure can govern information architecture while residual low-energy dynamics belong to a different universality class, especially the tricritical Ising \(c=7/10\) sector.

GHP is therefore not a claim that \(\phi\) numerically governs everything. Its strongest form is subtler: \(\phi\) may appear where a finite boundary becomes capable of stable self-reference, universal braiding, recoverable memory, and readable distinction. Dynamics can then live in residual sectors after architecture is factored out.

The observer side has two faces. Scientifically, the observer must become a formal boundary object: Markov blanket, conditional expectation \(E:M\to N\), code subspace, recoverable record, or shared-interface functional. Humanly, the observer is also the meaning layer: the place where fact becomes significance, record becomes memory, and event becomes myth. This second face is part of the phenomenon GHP is trying to protect, but it is not treated as physics evidence.

## 5. Mathematical Spine

Let \(D^2 = \sum_i d_i^2\) denote total quantum dimension.

**Minimality proposition.** In the class of unitary modular categories with at least one non-invertible simple object and braiding-universal computational power, Fibonacci is the minimum-cost architecture by total quantum dimension, with

\[
D^2 = 1+\phi^2 = 2+\phi.
\]

Fibonacci has two simple objects, \(1\) and \(\tau\), with fusion rule

\[
\tau \otimes \tau = 1 \oplus \tau.
\]

This gives

\[
d_\tau^2 = 1+d_\tau,
\]

so \(d_\tau=\phi\). The golden ratio enters as an algebraic consequence of the fusion rule, not as a numerical fit.

The mathematical and computational spine is not proprietary to GHP. The core ingredients belong to established external machinery: Fibonacci anyons support universal quantum computation by braiding alone in the Freedman-Larsen-Wang line of results; low-rank unitary modular category classification isolates Fibonacci as the relevant smallest non-pointed unitary modular category in the stated class; Ising has \(D^2=4\) but is excluded from the braiding-alone universality class because Ising braiding is not computationally universal without extra measurement-assisted resources. The mature version of GHP would need a proof arc, not just a beautiful synthesis.
