## Abstract

The Golden Horizon Principle is a variational observer-boundary conjecture: among admissible braiding-universal topological boundary architectures, Fibonacci appears as the minimal non-abelian architecture capable of universal computation by braiding alone. The strongest current core is categorical. Fibonacci has total quantum dimension

\[
D^2 = 1+\phi^2 = 2+\phi,
\]

and is the smallest braiding-universal non-abelian candidate inside the stated unitary modular-category class.

The stronger physical claim remains open: nature may select observer-boundary architectures by an extremal or balance principle whose lowest admissible non-abelian, self-referential, computationally universal closure is Fibonacci. The golden-chain computation supports the key architectural lesson: Fibonacci structure can govern information architecture while residual low-energy dynamics belong to a different universality class, especially the tricritical Ising \(c=7/10\) sector.

GHP is therefore strongest not as a claim that \(\phi\) numerically governs everything, but as a claim that Fibonacci may define the minimal readable boundary architecture while ordinary dynamics live in residual sectors after architecture is factored out. The next serious task is formal construction: the observer-boundary must become a mathematical object with a write law, finite-access projection, recoverability structure, and falsifiable bridge to ordinary physics.

## 5. Mathematical Spine

Let \(D^2 = \sum_i d_i^2\) denote total quantum dimension.

**Minimality proposition.** In the class of unitary modular categories with at least one non-invertible simple object and braiding-universal computational power, Fibonacci is the minimum-cost architecture by total quantum dimension, with

\[
D^2 = 1+\phi^2 = 2+\phi.
\]

Fibonacci has two simple objects, \(1\) and \(\tau\), with fusion rule

\[
\tau \otimes \tau = 1 \oplus \tau.
\]

This gives

\[
d_\tau^2 = 1+d_\tau,
\]

so \(d_\tau=\phi\). The golden ratio enters as an algebraic consequence of the fusion rule, not as a numerical fit.

The mathematical and computational spine is not proprietary to GHP. The core ingredients belong to established external machinery: Fibonacci anyons support universal quantum computation by braiding alone in the Freedman-Larsen-Wang line of results; low-rank unitary modular-category classification isolates Fibonacci as a central low-rank example in the stated class through Rowell-Stong-Wang; and Edie-Michell provides a complete classification of unitary fusion categories tensor-generated by an object of Fibonacci quantum dimension. Ostrik remains the clean rank-2 fusion-category anchor and Jones remains the canonical subfactor-index source. Together, these references harden the categorical lane: Fibonacci is not introduced by numerology but by established fusion/category machinery. The physical selection claim remains separate.

**Scope limit.** The categorical lane does not prove that nature selects Fibonacci. It identifies the mathematical minimum inside a stated class. The physical selection claim is separate.

### Fibonacci Quasiperiodic Driving as External Information-Protection Analogue

Dumitrescu et al. demonstrated an emergent dynamical symmetry-protected topological phase in a quasiperiodically driven trapped-ion quantum simulator using a Fibonacci pulse sequence. The phase dynamically protected edge qubits from control errors, cross-talk, and stray fields. This does not prove GHP, nor does it identify Fibonacci as a universal physical selector. It does, however, supply an external experimental analogue for a narrow GHP-compatible lesson: Fibonacci/quasiperiodic temporal structure can function as protective information architecture rather than mere aesthetic pattern.

## 8. Formal Boundary Machinery

The next hardening step is to turn observer-language into formal bridge objects.

The current bridge-object stack is:

- **Markov blanket:** statistical observer-boundary separating internal and external states through sensory and active states.
- **Conditional expectation \(E:M\to N\):** algebraic finite-access projection / useful forgetting from an ambient algebra to an observer-accessible subalgebra.
- **Regularized modular flow:** candidate route from continuous algebraic time to discrete write-events.
- **Write / Witness / Release:** operational action alphabet for memory-boundary behavior.
- **Reflected entropy / EWCS:** candidate shared-interface / consensus measure for two observers.
- **Holographic QEC:** recoverable boundary memory scaffold.

Kosaki extends Jones-style index theory to arbitrary factors and supplies a precise operator-algebraic setting in which conditional expectations can express structured access or coarse graining. Fewster develops the split property in locally covariant QFT in curved spacetime and gives a rigorous route for understanding local regions as subsystems. The conditional-expectation route and split-property route are bridge-object candidates for making finite observer access precise. They do not yet supply a derived write-law.

The sharpest near-term route is probably

\[
E_t:M_t\to N_t,
\]

a time-indexed conditional expectation whose thresholded or dissipative behavior could produce discrete write, witness, or release events. Naming that route is not the same as constructing it.

## 9. Shared-Interface And Recoverability

The strongest current vocabulary for a quantitative two-observer shared-interface functional comes from holographic information theory. Dutta-Faulkner gives the canonical-purification / reflected-entropy relation to the entanglement wedge cross-section; Jeong-Kim-Nishida studies first-order corrections to the reflected-entropy / EWCS relation; Tamaoka introduces odd entanglement entropy as another EWCS-related boundary quantity; and Babaei Velni, Mohammadi Mozaffar, and Vahidinia study several geometric and information-theoretic aspects of EWCS.

EWCS / reflected entropy therefore supplies the strongest current candidate vocabulary for a quantitative two-observer shared-interface functional. It is a bridge target, not a GHP result.

For recoverability, Verlinde-Verlinde frames black-hole information in quantum-error-correction terms, while Parikh-Verlinde provides finite-observer Hilbert-space context in de Sitter holography. These references support the recoverability lane as external machinery. They do not prove Fibonacci structure, GHP selection, or a GHP memory code.

## 10. VPH As GR-Side Anchor

The Viviani Phi Horizon / Surface is a metric-level Schwarzschild identity. It identifies the radius

\[
r = \phi r_s
\]

where the static-observer redshift factor equals the normalized radius under the chosen self-reference condition.

This is interesting because the same quadratic form \(x^2=x+1\) appears in the Fibonacci quantum-dimension equation and in the VPH fixed-point condition. But the same equation is not automatically the same object.

VPH is:

- a verified computation,
- a concrete GR-side golden fixed-point identity,
- a possible bridge target.

VPH is not:

- a technical event horizon,
- a null surface,
- a Killing horizon,
- a trapped surface,
- proof that nature selects Fibonacci,
- proof that GHP recovers GR, QFT, or the Standard Model.

Prior art matters here. Cruz-Olivares-Villanueva study golden-ratio structure in Schwarzschild-Kottler null-geodesic turning points; Coelho-Herdeiro study a golden-ratio critical merger of photon orbits in optical geometry for a relativistic Euler three-body black-hole setting; and Hod provides radius bounds for black-hole photonspheres. These results place golden-ratio and distinguished-radius structure in the black-hole literature, but they do not validate the VPH interpretation or the GHP framework.
