# IIT-GHP Bridge Audit

Toy-bridge audit only. Not proof of GHP. Not a paper upgrade.

## Bottom Line

IIT is a plausible **bridge candidate vocabulary** for GHP.

It is **not** currently a dynamics solution.

PCI is a useful **measurement analogy**.

It is **not** the write-law.

The strongest safe sentence is:

> IIT suggests that if consciousness depends on integrated and differentiated causal structure, then GHP may eventually need a write-law that selects stable, irreducible informational structure at an observer boundary.

That is as far as the bridge can be pushed right now.

## What Is Real Here

### 1. PCI is a real medical/neuroscience tool

The perturbational complexity index (PCI) is built from TMS-EEG perturbation experiments.

Simple version:

- perturb the cortex
- record the spatiotemporal response
- measure how complex and integrated the response is

It has been used in wakefulness, sleep, anesthesia, and disorders of consciousness.

Safe translation:

> conscious-like systems seem to produce responses that are neither purely local nor globally uniform.

### 2. IIT gives a real conceptual target

IIT says consciousness depends on integrated information and irreducible cause-effect structure.

Safe translation:

> if a system is conscious, its parts must matter together in a way that is not reducible to disconnected pieces.

That is relevant to GHP because GHP also needs a nontrivial observer-boundary write event, not just a passive container.

## What Is Not Safe

### 1. Capital `Phi` is not lowercase `phi`

This is the biggest immediate trap.

- IIT `Phi` / `Φ` = integrated information measure
- GHP `phi` / `φ` = golden ratio

The symbol overlap is not evidence.

No bridge may rely on the coincidence of notation.

### 2. PCI does not prove IIT in the strong mathematical sense

PCI is inspired by IIT-style ideas about integration and differentiation, but it is not the same thing as computing full IIT `Φ`.

Safe translation:

> PCI is a practical proxy for perturbational complexity, not a direct proof that IIT’s full formalism is correct.

### 3. IIT does not imply Fibonacci or golden topology

Nothing in the core IIT/PCI literature gives:

- Fibonacci fusion structure
- golden ratio architecture
- torus geometry
- observer-zero integer ladder

Those are extra hypotheses.

So the bridge cannot be:

> IIT needs golden ratio topology, therefore GHP is right.

That is a forced fit.

### 4. Fuzzball / Planck bounce / coma-state imports are too aggressive

The proposed “Gamma kernel patch” style language over-imports unrelated theories.

Unsafe jumps include:

- `Low_Phi_State` = coma = quantum bounce
- `Fuzzball_Cache` = literal boundary hard drive for conscious states
- IIT exclusion postulate = GHP write-law

Those are not derived bridges. They are speculative analogies.

## Strongest Criticisms To Keep In View

### 1. IIT is controversial

IIT is an active, disputed theory of consciousness.

Important critique classes:

- the `Phi` measure is difficult or ill-defined for general systems
- exclusion is controversial
- high integration may not be sufficient for consciousness
- strange or counterintuitive consciousness attributions remain a problem

So IIT cannot be used as settled ground.

### 2. GHP still lacks the actual write-law

Even if IIT were accepted, it would not automatically solve the paper’s main gap:

> how does information become physically written at the observer boundary?

IIT gives a target property:

- integrated irreducible structure

But it does not directly provide:

- the boundary transition rule
- the delay law
- the stabilization rule
- the release / recycle rule

### 3. The existing toy tests do not support a strong IIT-GHP bridge yet

Recent toy work supports only a weak structural claim:

> non-point memory-like structure matters more than point-slot storage

But the toys do **not** currently support:

- exact phi privilege
- exact phase-bound geometry
- exact local topology
- a clean perturbational consciousness bridge

So IIT should not be used to overstate what the toy already found.

## Best Use Of IIT For GHP

The best use is narrow and disciplined.

### Safe use

Use IIT/PCI as a **constraint language**:

- the write-law should not produce purely local isolated reads
- the write-law should not produce globally uniform mush
- the observer-boundary should likely support integrated but differentiated response

### Unsafe use

Do not use IIT/PCI as:

- proof of GHP
- proof of phi
- proof of torus geometry
- proof that reality is literally a hologram
- proof that consciousness is solved

## Current Verdict

### Structural Status

`Vulnerable but promising`

### The Gap

The bridge currently works only at the level of:

- information integration
- perturbation response
- irreducibility language

It does **not** yet work at the level of:

- mathematical derivation
- write-law closure
- physical boundary implementation

### The Fix

Treat IIT as a bridge candidate and audit target, not a solved bridge.

The next real work should be:

1. formalize the GHP write-law in information-theoretic terms
2. ask whether integrated/differentiated response is a consequence of that law
3. only then compare it to IIT/PCI-style measures

## Recommendation

Do not update the core share paper from IIT.

Do not harden the master from IIT.

Keep IIT in the ledger / research-note lane until one of these happens:

- GHP derives a boundary write-law that naturally yields an IIT-like constraint
- a new toy shows a robust perturbational signature that survives null pressure
- or a cleaner existing theory bridge appears in the literature

Right now IIT is worth studying, but not worth promoting.
