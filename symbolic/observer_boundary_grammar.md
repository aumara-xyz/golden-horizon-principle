# Observer-Boundary Grammar

## Status

Symbolic / conceptual grammar. Not theorem. Not physics evidence.

## The Boundary Object

The observer-boundary can be named several ways:

- finite readable surface,
- self-reference slot,
- useful hole,
- Markov blanket,
- code boundary,
- witness center,
- doorway,
- horizon,
- vessel-space.

These names are not identical technical claims. They are controlled metaphors around one recurring problem: how hidden structure becomes readable without flooding the observer.

## Slot, Signature, Surface

The compact public phrasing:

> The observer is the slot. Phi is the signature. The surface is where readability happens.

Meaning:

- `slot`: the admissibility opening where inside and outside can relate;
- `signature`: the minimal closure ratio or trace associated with the architecture;
- `surface`: the finite boundary through which records become available.

Guardrail:

> Do not say the observer is literally phi.

## Window, Vessel, Hull

Three useful metaphors:

- `Window`: lets reality become readable without admitting everything.
- `Vessel`: useful because of its hollow capacity, not only its walls.
- `Hull`: keeps the ocean out while allowing instruments to read it.

GHP reading:

> The boundary is not a wall against reality; it is the condition that lets reality be survived and read.

## Write, Witness, Release

The ternary symbolic alphabet:

- `+1 Write`: record, structure, light, durable memory.
- `0 Witness`: hold, useful emptiness, unresolved meaning, sacred pause.
- `-1 Release`: compress, demote, return, darkness.

Current engineering conclusion:

> Binary Ricci remains default. Witness is optional, targeted, and bounded.

## Publicness And Lived Reality

GHP distinguishes:

- public reality: stable enough to be shared or recorded;
- lived reality: what an observer actually metabolizes through context.

Two people can face the same public object and enter different lived worlds because their histories, priors, wounds, goals, and readiness differ.

Guardrail:

> This does not mean everyone creates their own physical universe.

## Safe Summary

The observer-boundary is the finite condition under which hidden structure becomes readable, writable, and meaningful without becoming total.
