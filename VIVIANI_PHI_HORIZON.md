# Viviani Phi Horizon / Surface

The Viviani Phi Horizon / Surface is a metric-level identity in Schwarzschild geometry. It identifies the radius

`r = phi r_s`

where the static-observer redshift factor equals the normalized radius under the chosen self-reference condition.

## What Is Computed

For a static observer in Schwarzschild geometry, the redshift factor depends on radius. Setting the observer condition so that the redshift expression equals the normalized radius picks out a unique positive solution, `r = phi r_s`.

## Why It Is Interesting

It is an exact golden fixed-point identity inside a standard GR background. That makes it a clean GR-side anchor for discussions about golden self-reference without pretending it settles the larger GHP program.

## Why It Is Not a Technical Event Horizon

This surface is outside the Schwarzschild event horizon and does not play the same role in causal structure. It is a metric identity, not a standard black-hole boundary in the technical sense.

## Why It Does Not Prove GHP

The identity shows a golden fixed point in a metric relation. It does not show that nature selects Fibonacci observer-boundaries, and it does not derive the selection principle.

## Relation To GHP

Within GHP, VPH is best read as a possible GR-side golden fixed-point anchor. It is suggestive and worth preserving, but it remains a metric-level bridge object rather than a proof of the wider framework.

## Guardrails

- Not a null surface.
- Not a Killing horizon.
- Not a trapped surface.
- Not proof that nature selects Fibonacci.
- Not proof of the Standard Model or quantum gravity.
